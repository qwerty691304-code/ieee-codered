1. FC BARCELONA TREBLE (2009) — THE 16% FIGURE
==================================================

- Source: Montesinos et al., "Barcelona baby boom: does sporting success
  affect birth rate?", BMJ, 2013;347:f7387.
- In May 2009, FC Barcelona won the treble: La Liga, Copa del Rey, and
  the UEFA Champions League.
- Researchers analyzed birth records (2007–2011) from two hospitals in
  the Bages/Solsonès region of Catalonia.
- Result: a 16% increase in births in February 2010 (nine months after
  the wins) compared with other years, and an 11% increase in March 2010.
- The city of Barcelona itself saw only a 1.2% increase over the same
  period — much smaller than the Bages/Solsonès region.
- Media at the time reported up to a 45% spike, dubbed the "Iniesta
  generation." The study authors stated this 45% figure was an
  exaggeration not supported by the hospital data they reviewed.
- The 16% figure is specific to this 2009 club-level treble win, not
  to the 2010 World Cup.

==================================================

- Match: Netherlands 0–1 Spain (after extra time)
- Date: 11 July 2010

3. 2010 WORLD CUP — BIRTH-RELATED RESEARCH
==================================================

- Source: Masukume & Grech, "The sex ratio at birth in South Africa
  increased 9 months after the 2010 FIFA World Cup," Early Human
  Development, 2015.
- This study examined the HOST country, South Africa — not Spain.
- It measured birth SEX RATIO (proportion of male births), not overall
  birth rate.
- Finding: February and March 2011 (about nine months after the World
  Cup) showed the highest observed sex ratio at birth for the 2003–2012
  period studied, a 0.63% increase (p = 0.02) versus the same months in
  2008–2012, corresponding to an estimated 1,000+ extra male births.
- This is a separate finding from the Barcelona 16% figure (Section 1):
  different event, different country, different metric (sex ratio vs.
  overall birth count).

==================================================
4. SPAIN — NATIONAL BIRTH TREND CONTEXT (SAME PERIOD)
==================================================

- Source: Spain's National Statistics Institute (INE).
- Spain recorded 519,779 births in 2008 (a 30-year high).
- Births then declined for five consecutive years, coinciding with the
  2008–2013 Spanish economic crisis.
- By 2014, annual births had fallen to 426,303 — roughly an 18% decline
  from the 2008 peak — before rising slightly (+0.1%) in 2014, the
  first annual increase after the five-year decline.
- Any localized football-related birth upticks (Sections 1 and 3) occurred
  against this broader national downward trend; they did not reverse it
  at the national level.
