2010 FIFA World Cup — Tournament & Host Facts (verified)

- South Africa was the first African nation to host the FIFA World Cup
  finals. South Africa was chosen as host back in 2004.

- The tournament ran from 11 June to 11 July 2010, across 10 venues in
  9 host cities.

- 32 teams competed, divided into 8 groups of 4, with the top two from
  each group advancing to a 16-team knockout stage.

- Opening match: South Africa vs Mexico, 11 June 2010, Soccer City,
  Johannesburg (ended 1-1; Siphiwe Tshabalala scored the hosts' opener).

- South Africa did not advance past the group stage — the first time
  a host nation failed to progress beyond the first round.

- The two finalists from the previous (2006) World Cup, Italy and
  France, were also both eliminated in the group stage of 2010.

- New Zealand was the only team in the tournament that went unbeaten
  in all of its matches (three draws), though they were eliminated in
  the group stage on goal difference.

- Total goals scored across the tournament: 145, averaging 2.27 goals
  per match — the lowest goals-per-match figure of any 64-match World
  Cup format up to that point.

- Total official attendance across the tournament: 3,178,856, averaging
  49,670 per match.

- FIFA estimated that roughly 3.2 billion people worldwide watched at
  least part of the 2010 World Cup, around 46% of the global population
  at the time.

- The official match ball was the Adidas Jabulani — Zulu for "to
  celebrate." It was developed with Loughborough University's Sports
  Technology Institute and was widely criticized by players and
  goalkeepers as unpredictable in flight.

- A special gold version of the Jabulani, called the "Jo'bulani" (a
  nod to "Jo'burg"), was produced exclusively for the final.

- The vuvuzela, a plastic horn associated with South African football
  culture, became one of the tournament's defining and most debated
  sounds. It was later banned at subsequent UEFA and other competitions.

- Final standings: Champions Spain (1st title), runners-up Netherlands,
  third place Germany, fourth place Uruguay.

- Closing ceremony performers included Shakira, who performed the
  official tournament anthem "Waka Waka (This Time for Africa)," and
  South African group Ladysmith Black Mambazo.

- Nelson Mandela made a brief appearance at the closing ceremony before
  the final, despite having missed the opening ceremony due to the
  death of his great-granddaughter in a road accident days earlier.

Source basis: FIFA tournament records, Wikipedia "2010 FIFA World Cup"
and "2010 FIFA World Cup final" summary pages.
